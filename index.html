Research

LightSeg: Lightweight Fetal Ultrasound Segmentation

Developing efficient and interpretable segmentation models using hybrid loss design, architectural optimization, and explainability methods such as GradCAM++, with the goal of improving reliability and clinical usability in fetal ultrasound imaging.

AI for Synthetic EHR Generation (Duke University)

At Duke University's Kamaleswaran Lab, I contributed to building large-scale clinical data pipelines and structured temporal representations to support synthetic EHR generation. My work involved engineering robust feature extraction workflows, supporting VAE–GAN based architectures, and assisting with fidelity and utility evaluations. A co-authored abstract based on this work has been accepted for presentation, reflecting our collaborative effort to advance safe and reliable AI systems for clinical research.

BanglaSER: Speech Emotion Recognition

Designing emotion recognition models using hybrid spectral–temporal features, convolutional and recurrent networks, and fusion-based approaches to improve performance in low-resource language settings such as Bangla.
